Genre: Philosophy

Target group: Philosophy-interested; advanced lay people as well as experts.

Influence of information on judgment

In order for the evolutionary process to take place, the information must have an influence on the judge's judgment. Or, in other words, if the information makes it possible to influence the judge's judgement, then the information that has been sent is more viable. And as we have already seen, this is the case in all our examples. The gene has an influence on the speed, strength and intelligence of the host and thus also on its chances of survival. This is always an influence and no guarantee. People also uninstall good programs or animals die because they had bad luck. However, this small influence is usually sufficient to stabilize information for a long time. Because even if someone uninstalls a good program, even though it is good, then it will be used by others. Although the program has only a small influence on the user, it is enough that it is distributed throughout the world, there are always users who use the program.

Judges usually change steadily

The judge changes all the time. This is not a problem, but the fact that allows things to evolve. This is achieved by a relative evaluation system and not absolute. There are not the programs installed on a computer that allow to write twice as fast as the typewriter, but the program that allows to write the fastest. This increases the measuring bar with every new best program. This increases the pressure for other programs to develop. The advantage of this kind of judge is also that there are always those who survive. Because one is always best. Not good, but best.

Evolution is simply analyzed. In a world without carnivores, camouflage is hardly relevant. But with it. The judge ‹Natur› therefore always decides relative to everything else. Whether a bird can survive is also due to the bird population. And the amount of food. And if people come and build roads at once, then birds that crack nuts using cars have an advantage.

So we see that the verdict of whether a pattern can survive changes. And the judgment always refers to the past. So nature does not say in one day that the animals survive with the best camouflage and the next with the worst camouflage. But today's judgment always has something to do with yesterday's judgment. You can imagine there are fewer carnivores and that's why camouflage is less important. Just like a computer. If the user only randomly installs programs, the quality of the programs will not improve. It only improves when it always replaces a worse one with a better one. These examples show that it is irrelevant whether the change is targeted, as with the computer, or randomly as with evolution. At the first, computer scientists work to develop the program as well as an administrator who has a goal and uses the best programs for it. Evolution works with random processes. Both guarantee progress. As long as there is a judge, species will adapt and programs will become better. There is no way that this cannot happen(8).
